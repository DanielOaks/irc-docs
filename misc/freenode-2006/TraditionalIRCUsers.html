TraditionalIRCUsers

From FreenodeDevelopmentWiki

This page describes the culture of a group of people Rob Levin has termed "traditional IRC users." The culture figures prominently in general purpose IRC networks, and its core values are not well-aligned with the purpose and intent of Freenode.

Please see the discussion page for comments and proposed changes.

Anarchy and Conflict

IRC is regarded as being essentially anarchic. Anarchy is not viewed primarily as a cooperative state, but rather as a state of conflict. As such, traditional IRC users frequently view the quality of IRC as proportional to the amount of active conflict a network can contain and withstand.

Lack of External Reference

IRC is considered to be self-contained and not part of "real life."

Values Neutrality

Attempts to reflect specific values or ethical constraints in the policies and operation of an IRC network are considered to be an inappropriate imposition.

Lack of Serious Purpose

Attempts to use IRC for any serious purpose are considered inappropriate and futile.

"Professional Hobby" Orientation

IRC administration is considered by traditional administrators to be a professional-quality effort in service to a hobby. Suggestions of any costs associated with bandwidth or supervision are considered to be evidence of an inappropriate or unrealistic mindset.

Policy by Cultural Fiat

Traditional IRC users ignore statements of network policy whenever possible and maintain the belief that IRC tradition always overrides local network policies whenever the two are in conflict.

Broad Communication Considered Abusive

Global notices to users for purposes other than providing emergency routing information are considered abusive.

Fragmentation Considered Desirable

Attempts to foster or encourage a network-wide sense of community are considered inappropriate and intrusive.

Anonymity Considered Essential

Personal anonymity is considered to be an essential feature of the IRC experience. Incidents in which such anonymity is abused are considered to be strong indirect evidence of the proper functioning of the IRC environment.

Allocation by Current Control

Traditional IRC users consider any framework for allocating IRC nicknames or channels other than "first-come, first-served" to be an inappropriate imposition. In particular, any channel allocation system which honors claims to the use of a channel name which are derived from outside IRC is considered to be contrary to the spirit of IRC culture.
