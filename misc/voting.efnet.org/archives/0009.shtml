Topic: Definition of majority and what is required for a vote to pass
Called by: madmax (irc.ins.net.uk)
Seconded by: grievah (irc.isdnet.fr)
Called on: 03/13/01
Ended on: 03/27/01
Status: PASSED (yes: 19, no: 0, abstain: 3, elig: ~30)

Description:


We originally voted on:
"1) Definition of a majority vote
A vote will be considered "passed" if: 
(number of yes votes) / (number of cast votes) >= 51%
provided that at least 51% of eligible servers cast a vote."

However this would make a No vote equal to an Abstaination from voting.

The proposal is to superceed the previous agreement with this:

Definition of a (simple) Majority vote:
A vote will be considered "passed" by majority vote if:
(number of YES votes) / (number of YES and NO votes) >= 51%
provided that at least 51% of eligible servers cast a YES or NO vote)

Therefore abstaining is (as far as a count is concerned) the same as not turning up, but
without the blackmark against the admin for the lack of vote.

This is a vote purely for the definition of a simple majority (as in the default voting result
requirements).  We may decide at a later date that some decisions require their own system, for
example a 2/3rd majority or quorum.
