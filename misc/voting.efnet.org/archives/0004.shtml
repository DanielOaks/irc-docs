Purpose: To set guidelines for hostname spoofing.

Content: A server admin may only spoof domains for which they or the end-user 
has ownership (i.e. billing contact for) of said domain. Spoofing non-existant 
domains shall be allowed. However, if it becomes known to the admin or user 
that the domain has been taken or under ownership of a person other than 
end-user or admin and niether party has obtained permission from the domain 
owner, said spoof shall be revoked.

Additionally, spoofing non-existant tld's shall be allowed provided fake tld 
does not exceed 4 characters. (i.e. 5 chars or more is deemed illegal).

The admin takes responsibility for any spoofs on their server and should 
revoke immediately upon notification of abuse from a "trusted" party. (i.e. 
an oper)

Reasoning: The top portion is self-explanitory. The TLD portion comes from 
a concern that I have seen long faked tld's mess with monitor bots and 
scripts/clients in unpredictable ways. The final part, again should be 
common sense.

That should do it unless I forgot something or went too far over the edge.
